About

We document how language lives

Slangist is a living archive of internet slang. From viral classics to freshly coined expressions. We believe language is culture, and culture deserves documentation.

✦
Our Mission

Language changes faster than dictionaries can keep up

Oxford updates once a year. The internet updates every hour. Slangist exists in the gap between them.

Every meme, every viral comment, every WhatsApp chain. All of it contributes to a living language that formal dictionaries are always a year too late to document. We catch these words at the moment they happen.

Our goal isn't just to define slang. It's to document the full cultural moment around each word: the origin, the context, the community that birthed it, and why it resonated. That's what separates Slangist from a Wikipedia stub.

Featured Word

Meet Velkāmya, a word we helped coin

Most slang dictionaries document words after they go viral. We believe in documenting words before. Language needs witnesses, not just historians.

Velkāmya (वेलकाम्य) is a 2026 Hinglish slang word that means "you're welcome" — coined by @avgec to fill a gap that Hindi never had. Every existing option (koi baat nahi, swagat hai) either dismissed gratitude or sounded like a hotel lobby. Velkāmya fixed that.

Our Standards

How we document a word

Every entry in Slangist follows the same rigorous documentation standard:

Step 1
Word is identified

From social media observations, community submissions, or organic discovery. We look for words that solve a real communication need or capture a cultural moment.

Step 2
Origin is researched

We trace the word to its earliest documented use: the post, the creator, the community. We don't publish without knowing where it came from.

Step 3
Context is documented

Cultural context, usage patterns, related words, pronunciation. We document the full picture, not just a definition.

Step 4
Published with care

Each page is crafted to be genuinely useful. Not just SEO bait. Real examples. Real context. Real culture.
